Illumiventory

A UX/UI Case Study on Non-profit Inventory Management

The Challenge

Many non-profit organizations rely on outdated, manual inventory tracking methods, leading to inefficiencies, difficulty in locating items, and a lack of visibility for available resources. Our client was a a non-profit dedicated to providing free clothing, furniture, language classes, job search programs, and other essential goods and services to new immigrants. The primary issue they faced was that volunteers and staff had no standardized process for tracking donations, leading to frequent mismanagement and frustration.

Furthermore, the existing process heavily relied on handwritten logs and a WhatsApp group, making it difficult to maintain real-time inventory accuracy. The lack of a structured digital system meant that essential resources were either underutilized or went unnoticed.

Understanding the Users

We conducted user research through interviews with volunteers at the organization to identify key pain points. Findings revealed that:

  • Volunteers struggled to track available inventory, leading to inefficiencies in distribution.
  • There was no way to categorize or filter items, making it time-consuming to find needed supplies.
  • The director, who handled most logistics, was overburdened with managing records manually.

Through empathy mapping, we identified that the core needs of the users were:

  • An easy-to-use system with minimal learning curve.
  • Clear tracking of available and checked-out inventory.
  • Access to item history to prevent overstocking or duplicate requests.

Designing the Solution

Through iterative wireframing and prototyping in Figma, we developed an inventory system that prioritizes accessibility, efficiency, and real-time updates.

The core features included:

  • QR Scanning: To quickly add and remove items from inventory.
  • Visual Categorization: Items organized into predefined categories for easier browsing.
  • Check-in/Check-out System: Volunteers could track who had taken an item and when it was returned.

User Testing & Refinements

We conducted usability testing sessions with volunteers and staff to evaluate the prototype. Key insights led to the following refinements:

  • Simplified UI to reduce cognitive load.
  • Increased button sizes for easier interaction on mobile devices.
  • Integrated search and filtering to allow quick item look-up.
  • Added color-coded labels for item status (e.g., available, reserved, checked-out).

These changes improved the overall user experience, making the system more intuitive and efficient.

Final UI Design

Our final product is an intuitive, mobile-friendly digital inventory system with:

  • Real-time tracking and status updates.
  • Automated low-stock alerts to notify when supplies need replenishing.
  • A user-friendly dashboard providing a snapshot of available inventory.

Impact & Future Considerations

Illumiventory successfully transformed an inefficient manual process into a structured digital solution, significantly improving tracking and reducing administrative burden.

Future iterations may include:

  • AI-powered inventory suggestions based on usage trends.
  • Automated notifications for expiring or low-stock items.
  • Integration with donation tracking for automated reporting.

With further development, Illumiventory has the potential to be implemented across various small non-profits, improving efficiency and resource distribution.
