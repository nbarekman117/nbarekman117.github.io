Wikipedia Redesign

Streamlined Wikipedia's complex IA and UI

Challenge Overview

The Wikipedia platform is one of the most-visited websites globally, but its design has changed little over time. My goal was to identify usability and aesthetic pain points across key pages, propose and prototype improvements while maintaining Wikipedia's core mission of providing free, accessible knowledge. Focus areas included reducing information overload, clarifying visual hierarchy, improving accessibility, and enhancing engagement without losing trust or familiarity.

Homepage Redesign

Pain Points

  • Overwhelming, cluttered layout with excessive links and images.
  • Lack of personalization or dynamic content recommendations.
  • Visual clutter from random color use and inconsistent grouping.
  • Limited mobile optimization and lack of strong visual hierarchy.
  • Accessibility issues due to low contrast and small font sizes.

Solutions

I redesigned the homepage using a structured grid system and a clean grayscale color palette, creating a calmer visual experience. Key features included:

  • Dynamic sections like "Trending" and "On This Day" for fresher content discovery.
  • Accessible search prominently featured at the top.
  • Improved hierarchy with larger headings, simplified navigation, and condensed link lists.
  • Hover effects to reduce color noise and emphasize interaction points.
  • Enhanced contrast ratios and larger font sizes to meet accessibility standards.

Content Page Redesign: Gender Representation in Video Games

Pain Points

  • Text-heavy design leading to readability issues and cognitive fatigue.
  • Hidden navigation tools and dense table of contents structure.
  • Lack of visual aids to complement long sections of text.

Solutions

To improve the reading experience, I reorganized the content for clarity and simplicity:

  • Condensed and merged redundant sections in the table of contents.
  • Added infographics and charts to break up text and visualize key data.
  • Introduced an accessibility toolbar with text resizing, contrast toggling, and read-aloud functions.
  • Applied consistent margins, spacing, and highlight colors to create a cohesive visual experience across pages.

Help Page Redesign

Pain Points

  • Fragmented information spread across multiple disconnected pages.
  • Lack of visual guidance for users needing help with editing, accounts, or browsing.

Solutions

The redesigned Help Page prioritized user needs through structured FAQs, clear sections, and a visual-first design:

  • Grouped support topics under clear categories (Accounts, Editing, Research).
  • Prominent search bar and quick link tiles for faster access.
  • Consistency with homepage visual style to maintain trust and reduce learning curve.
  • Help resources verified through brief usability testing to ensure clarity and accessibility.

Final Reflections

This project reinforced my commitment to user-centered design principles. Every redesign decision balanced improved usability, visual appeal, and accessibility without compromising Wikipedia’s foundational values. By applying iterative testing, accessibility checks (using tools like WebAim contrast checker), and thoughtful information architecture strategies, I crafted an experience that feels modern yet familiar — empowering all users to access and engage with knowledge easily.
