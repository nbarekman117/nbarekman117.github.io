Social Media Engagement Research

My position at the LAMA (Language, Attention, Music, and Audition) lab originally started as a part of the ROP (Research Opportunity Program), where I designed and conducted a study to improve community engagement and participant recruitment for research labs. After this program ended, I was hired back as their Social Media Manager, where I continued collecting data and creating content for the lab, ensuring a balance between sharing scientific knowledge and staying on top of comedy trends to appeal to family study participants and university students alike.

My Research

As part of a Summer Undergraduate Research Fair (SURF), I conducted a study exploring how various social media strategies influence user engagement for academic research labs. Focusing on the LAMA Lab at UTM, I examined the effects of different platforms (Instagram vs. Facebook), content types (static images vs. reels), thematic categories (educational vs. social content), and the presence of human faces.

Over a 10-week period, we posted 26 times and collected metrics including likes, reach, and new followers. Posts were controlled for style and timing, with consistent scheduling and hashtag usage. Results showed that Instagram posts, especially static images, had higher engagement overall. Facebook, meanwhile, performed better with educational content. Our findings suggest that platform choice, content format, and human presence can shape engagement, with implications for how research labs can more effectively reach their communities.
